Partners and Project Background

The HAB Monitoring data shared on this dashboard are the result of a collaborative effort between the Water Boards and Restore the Delta. This monitoring program aims to address the growing concern over HABs in the Delta region raised by community organizations and tribes.

Restore the Delta

Restore the Delta (RTD) is a nonprofit organization whose mission is to ensure the health of the San Francisco Bay-Delta Estuary and Delta Communities. RTD established their HABs monitoring program in 2021 in an effort to increase awareness of HABs alongside other water quality issues in the Delta. Restore the Delta manages all aspects of field work for this monitoring program ranging from recruiting and training field staff, to collecting water samples and field data.

Central Valley Water Board

Adressing HABs in the City of Stockton Waters was formalized as a Central Valley Water Board (CVWB) priority in the 2021 Triennial Review ( Project 30 ). The first steps for this project as outlined in the Triennial Review were for CVWB staff to establish partnerships with community members and stakeholders and to identify funding resources. Central Valley Water Board began a HAB monitoring partnership with Restore the Delta in 2022. Funding sources for laboratory analysis of HAB samples have included the Central Valley Water Board, State Water Board Freshwater and Estuarine Harmful Algal Bloom (FHAB) Program and the Division of Water Rights.

The Central Valley Water Board created this dashboard to share laboratory microcystin data from this monitoring program with the public and other agencies. Statewide HAB recreational advisories can be found on the Water Board's HAB Reports Map.

State Water Resources Control Board

Multiple divisions within the State Water Resources Control Board (SWRCB) have funded laboratory analysis of HAB samples for this monitoring program. In 2022 and 2023, funding for cyanotoxin laboratory analysis was provided by both the Central Valley Water Board and the State Water Board FHAB Program. Beginning in 2024, laboratory funding has been fully provided by the State Water Board Division of Water Rights.
